Addendum A

Path to the 99-year lease

A step-by-step path to securing long-term lease security on Agua Caliente tribal trust land — a core structural pillar of the transaction.

Recommended Path

01
Obtain a Title Status Report (TSR) from BIA Palm Springs Agency to confirm land status, identify landowners, and surface any encumbrances.
02
Determine governing framework: Agua Caliente HEARTH Act regulations or standard BIA 25 CFR Part 162 (Subpart D) approval path.
03
Engage tribal realty office and BIA early — request a Preliminary Review of proposed lease terms under §162.440 before formal submission.
04
Negotiate new 99-year lease with the individual allottee landowner. This is a new lease (not an extension) — the cleanest path.
05
Secure Indian landowner consent and prepare full lease package per 25 CFR §162.438: executed lease, valuation, NEPA, entity docs, insurance, performance bond, and environmental assessments.
06
BIA reviews complete package within 30 days (§162.440). If approved, lease is recorded at LTRO.
07
Concurrent assignment: Seller assigns existing leasehold to LBHG entity while LBHG applies as new leaseholder for the 99-year term, avoiding any gap in control.
08
Initiate NEPA environmental assessment early (Month 1–2). BIA may adopt an existing EA/EIS under 43 CFR 46.320. Hotel continuation of use simplifies NEPA.
09
Engage MAI appraiser with BIA trust land experience for fair market rent determination. Validate current ground lease terms against comparable Agua Caliente leases.
10
Lender coordination: SBA/conventional lenders require recorded lease exceeding loan term. A 99-year lease satisfies all institutional requirements. Target: BIA approval and LTRO recording before refi application.

Two-Phase Approach

A signed PSA or definitive purchase-option package is sufficient to begin groundwork immediately. The formal BIA application is tied to concurrent assignment and purchase-option exercise.

Phase 1: Groundwork (Years 1–2)

During the building lease, begin all preparatory work:

Get the TSR from BIA
Identify the landowner and build the relationship
Commission environmental assessment (NEPA)
Get the MAI appraisal for rent reasonableness
Pre-negotiate lease terms with the landowner
Request BIA Preliminary Review

None of this requires control of the ground lease. It is preparatory work that materially de-risks the formal application.

Phase 2: Formal Application (Year 3)

Begin the formal 99-year application ~12 months before target refi:

Exercise the purchase option
Harry assigns existing ground lease to LBHG entity
Submit full lease package to BIA (prep work done)
BIA approval within 30 days for complete packages
LTRO records the new 99-year lease
Apply for SBA/conventional refi with recorded lease

With groundwork complete, the formal application should be materially faster and cleaner than a cold-start process.

Key insight: Seller receives $500K upfront, LBHG covers the landlord-cost stack through the lease structure, and the remaining $3.5M carries at zero interest. That window is used to renovate, stabilize, and advance the 99-year lease file. By the time the purchase option is exercised, the BIA package is substantially prepared and the SBA refi can be paired with the acquisition takeout.

Addendum D

Why this structure wins

Building Lease with Purchase Option — the case for operating first and buying later.

The economics favor patience

Harry has $4M of existing bank debt and $4M of equity. LBHG pays $500K upfront, services the $4M debt through rent, and leaves the remaining $3.5M at zero interest for three years — saving roughly $720K versus market and keeping more capital inside the repositioning.

Under an accelerated purchase, the deal takes on ~$12M in SBA debt from Day 1, implying $906–928K of annual debt service against Year 1 NOI of only $162K. The structured path keeps effective debt service at ~$350K/year — light enough for reserves to absorb the ramp.

We don't own during the transition

Years 1–2 carry the most risk: construction delays, permit issues, slower occupancy, supply chain surprises. Under this structure, exposure is limited to operating losses and invested capex — not the full $8M purchase. This is the core investor protection: the building lease allows the concept to be proven before ownership is assumed.

The SBA conversation is completely different in Year 3

By Year 3, the SBA lender evaluates a recorded 99-year lease, 12+ months of stabilized NOI at $1.6M+, a completed renovation, and a clean operating track record. That is a fundamentally stronger underwriting case than showing up in Year 1 with projections and an unfinished BIA process. The result: a cleaner credit story, stronger proceeds, and a more financeable refinance.

When accelerated purchase becomes the play

The accelerated path becomes logical if Seller prefers a faster exit. If that occurs, the LOI preserves optionality by keeping the purchase path intact while referencing the operating structure as a fallback. Absent that signal, the structured acquisition remains stronger: lower risk, lower debt service through the J-curve, proven NOI at refinance, and approximately $680–840K of interest savings.
